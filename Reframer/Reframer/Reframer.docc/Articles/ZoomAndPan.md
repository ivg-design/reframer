# Zoom and Pan

Inspect video details at any magnification.

## Overview

Zoom in to examine fine details, zoom out to see the full picture, and pan around to focus on specific areas. Zoom always anchors from the top-left corner of the video for consistent positioning.

## Zooming

### Scroll Wheel

When the window is unlocked:

| Action | Gesture |
|--------|---------|
| Zoom (5% steps) | **Shift + Scroll** |
| Fine zoom (0.1% steps) | **⌘ Shift + Scroll** |

### Zoom Input

For precise control, type the exact zoom percentage:

1. Click the zoom field in the control bar (shows **100%**)
2. Type your desired percentage (10–1000)
3. Press **Enter**

Arrow keys while focused:

- **↑ / ↓** — Step by 1%
- **Shift ↑ / ↓** — Step by 10%
- **⌘ ↑ / ↓** — Step by 0.1%

## Panning

### Arrow Keys

When unlocked, arrow keys pan the video:

| Action | Shortcut |
|--------|----------|
| Pan 1 pixel | **← → ↑ ↓** |
| Pan 10 pixels | **Shift + arrows** |
| Pan 100 pixels | **Shift ⌘ + arrows** |

### Mouse Drag

Click and drag anywhere on the video to pan. This works at any zoom level.

## Zoom Display

The overlay in the upper-right corner shows current zoom:

```
125%
```

This appears when zoomed above or below 100%.

## Reset View

| Action | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Reset zoom only | Press **0** |
| Reset zoom and pan | Press **R** or click the **↺** button |

## Tips

- **Zoom for detail work** — Zoom in to trace fine lines or check alignment
- **Pan to reposition** — Use arrow keys for precise pixel-level positioning
- **Reset quickly** — Press **R** to snap back to the default view
- **Use fine zoom** — ⌘+Shift+Scroll gives 0.1% precision for exact matching
